Bio

I graduated in September 2012 from both, the School Of Engineering, Applied Mathematics and Computing Group of Cranfield University (UK) and ESTIA Institute of Technology (France), with an MSc in Computational & Software Techniques in Engineering, specialising in Computer Aided Engineering. After having successfully completed both degrees and undertook my 6-month end-of-course internship within the Prognostics and Health Management field at PHM Technology, in Melboure; I decided to join the LISMMA research center of SUPMECA engineering school in Paris. My main mission was to focus on the use of Dassault Système's Model-Based Systems Engineering solution, ENOVIA-CATIA RFLP V6 (3D Experience), for the modelling and simulation of dynamic multi-engineering systems. Although it truly was an enriching experience, my key interest for applied research led me to Arts & Métiers ParisTech engineering school in Aix-en-Provence (France) where I worked as a Ph.D candidate at the Information and Systems Science Laboratory (LSIS) UMR CNRS 7296. My Ph.D focused on the application of data science techniques including machine learning, text mining, natural language processing, graph theory, and visual analytics to enable subcontractors to gain insight and discover opportunities in a massive set of text-based requirements. To date, I am a postdoctoral research fellow in Model-Based Systems Engineering at Airbus Helicopters on behalf of the Information and Systems Science Laboratory (LSIS) UMR CNRS 7296. My main missions are : (1) To prove that the MBSE method "Property Model Methodology (PMM)" is a promising simulation-based solution to enhance the specification, validation, design, and verification of technical systems through an experimentation on two main helicopter sub-systems with the MathWorks modelling and simulation suite; (2) to capitalise the know-how related to the application of the MBSE PMM method and its implementation with the MathWorks modelling and simulation suite; (3) to coach systems design responsibles who are eager to adopt the PMM approach. From a broader perspective, my researches concentrate on systems engineering, product lifecycle management, and data science to support the specification and design of complex systems.
