Bio

I am an Assistant Professor at Arts & Métiers ParisTech and a research fellow at the Physical & Virtual Systems Engineering Laboratory (LISPEN EA). My research concentrates on methods and tools to efficiently engineer complex socio-technical systems by combining aspects of design theory, systems engineering, modelling and simulation, and data science. I began as a R&T engineer in model-based systems engineering at the LISMMA research laboratory of SUPMECA engineering school, France. I have an MSc in Computational and Software Techniques in Engineering with the specialist option Computer Aided Engineering from the Applied Mathematics and Computing Group of Cranfield University, a Ph.D in Product Design from Arts & Métiers ParisTech in 2016, and I was a post-doctoral research fellow in simulation-based systems engineering at Airbus Helicopters on behalf of the Science & Systems Laboratory (LSIS) UMR CNRS 7296. I am an active member of the French chapter of INCOSE (AFIS) and the French PLM Lab association, as well as a member of the Design Society.
