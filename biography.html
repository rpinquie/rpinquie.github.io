About me...

I graduated in September 2012 from both, the School Of Engineering, Applied Mathematics and Computing Group of Cranfield University (UK), and 'École Supérieure des Technologies Industrielles Avancées' (France) with an MSc in Computational & Software Techniques in Engineering, option Computer Aided Engineering.

During my studies, I had the opportunity to undertake several internships within the Computer-Aided Engineering sector; i.e. Computer-Aided Design, Mechanical Engineering, Stress Analysis and Reliability Engineering.

After having successfully completed both degrees and undertook my 6-month end-of-course internship within the Prognostics and Health Management field at PHM Technology, in Melboure; I decided to join the 'Laboratoire d'Ingénierie des Systèmes Mécaniques et des MAtériaux' research center, of the 'Institut Supérieur de Mécanique de Paris' - SupMéca engineering school in Paris.

Specialties

My activities include the modelling, design, simulation as well as analysis of mechanical and multi-physics phenomena for both academic and research projects.

Currently, my main mission is to focus on the use of Dassault Système's software, ENOVIA CATIA V6, for the design, modelling and simulation of complex dynamic multi-engineering systems. This activity requires several skills such as Solid Mechanics, Control Systems, CAD, PLM, Scientific Programming and Model-Based Systems Engineering.
