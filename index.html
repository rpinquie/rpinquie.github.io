Bio

I graduated in September 2012 from both, the School Of Engineering, Applied Mathematics and Computing Group of Cranfield University (UK) and ESTIA Institute of Technology (France), with an MSc in Computational & Software Techniques in Engineering, specialising in Computer Aided Engineering. After having successfully completed both degrees and undertook my 6-month end-of-course internship within the Prognostics and Health Management field at PHM Technology, in Melboure; I decided to join the LISMMA research center of SUPMECA engineering school in Paris. My main mission was to focus on the use of Dassault Système's Model-Based Systems Engineering solution, ENOVIA-CATIA V6, for the modelling and simulation of dynamic multi-engineering systems. Although it truly was an enriching experience, my key interest for applied research led me to Arts & Métiers ParisTech engineering school in Aix-en-Provence (France) where I am currently a Ph.D candidate at the Information and Systems Science Laboratory - LSIS UMR CNRS 7296. My Ph.D focuses on the application of data science including machine learning, text mining, natural language processing, graph theory, and visual analytics to enable subcontractors to gain insight and discover opportunities in a massive set of text-based requirements. From a broader perspective, I concentrate on systems engineering, product lifecycle management and data science to support the design of complex systems.
